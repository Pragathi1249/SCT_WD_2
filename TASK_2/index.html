A few things worth remembering

Order isn't optional

This calculator works left-to-right with the last operator pressed taking effect on the next number — type a full line like 12+5 or 8×9−3 and press = when you're ready. It won't silently guess what you meant; that's the whole point of a ledger.

Percentage means "of the last number"

Pressing % divides the current number by 100 in place — handy for tips and discounts. Try 200×15% to see 15% of 200, or just 50% on its own to get 0.5.

Dividing by zero won't break anything

Try 5÷0 — instead of crashing, the display calmly says Error, and a single tap on C clears the slate. The same happens for anything malformed, like two operators in a row.

Your keyboard works too

Numbers, + − × ÷ (use * / on a keyboard), . and % all type directly. Enter solves the line, Backspace erases one character, and Escape wipes the page clean.

One decimal point per number

The calculator quietly ignores a second . in the same number, so 12.5.5 simply stays 12.5 — no need to watch for it yourself.
